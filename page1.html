Walkthrough

After the student has taken his exam, the student follows the follow lines to a new room, where he or she can use the installation. The student approaches the installation and sees one wall with an opening. The contrast created between the white wall and the black opening creates a kind of illusion. This arouses curiosity, one does not know what it actually is until one comes closer and the opening becomes visible. Follow the follow lines on the ground that lead to the entrance of the installation.
There is a notch along the opening on the left, where the hands can be disinfected with disinfectant. Here are also some instructions written out:

Disinfect
Enter
Take
Explore
Shoot
Take

The student reads the word "DETEST" with each initial letter from top to bottom. This correlates with abhorrence of the corona crisis. The student then disinfects his hands and can enter the room. You are guided inwards by a sloping wall.

At the entrance, a signal will also be given when you can enter the installation. This by means of an entrance light, the word "shoot" will light up red when one can enter the installation, and be dimmed when someone is already in the installation.

Once inside, you enter a dark corridor with a white notch in the wall at the end. Here one can take a paint ball by an automatic system. He or she is then led to the right and thus enters a completely white space.

In this, the student sees an installation, consisting of two posts, permanently mounted in the ground with a fixed reusable mouth mask (the catapult) that will help shoot the paint balls. In the middle of this construction the word "shoot" is written on the ground. This refers to taking your shot.

The visitor then places the paint ball in the mask, while the mask is tightened, one thinks of all the frustration, emotion that one wants to get rid of. And then shoots the dot on the canvas that is right in front of them.

Snapshots are taken when the student performs the action. A number of cameras are placed in the different corners of the installation. After the action one steps further and thus ends up at the second corridor. There is also a white notch here where you will receive a card with the photos taken of the moment. With this we want to leave the visitor an impression and at the same time leave a imprint on our installation.

When the visitor subsequently leaves the installation, there is a notch on the outside. Where you can disinfect your hands again. Above the notch is also a subtle omission described as "destressed", something that for us is a response to the whole experience of the installation. Frustration and stress are lost. This allows you to face new challenges with fresh and positive thoughts.

The canvas in the installation is removed when it is full of paint splatters and can be replaced with a new blank canvas. This routine is done about weekly so that enough people can continue to visit the installation and everyone can use their expression. At the end of the exam period, the canvases will be exhibited in the various campuses at the University of Antwerp. This as a memorabilia of their imprint that they left on the installation and of a difficult exam and corona period. Through interaction and teamwork, the students managed to complete this together.
